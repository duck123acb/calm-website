Areas of Growth
  • Showing Enthusiasm - Employers want to hire people who seem excited about the job. Smile, engage, and let them see your interest!

Strengths
  • Well-Structured Answers - Your answers were clear, well-organized, and on topic. You gave just the right amount of detail to fully answer the questions.
  • Stayed on Topic - You kept your answers focused and didn't go off on unrelated tangents. This made your responses stronger and easier to follow.

Reflection

Feedback

The feedback challenged my initial thoughts about the interview. I believed I had done well in presenting my qualifications, but I didn't realize how important it is to actively show enthusiasm. After reflecting, I see how important energy and interest are in making a positive impression. Moving forward, this feedback has made me more aware of my nonverbal communication and tone, and I'm more committed to showing genuine interest in future interviews.



Next Steps

In general the main thing I should do is practice showing enthusiasm. I should:

  • Practice mock interviews with a friend or a mirror and focus on using more facial expressions (like smiling) and an energetic tone.
  • Record myself answering questions and adjust myself accordingly
